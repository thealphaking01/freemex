 FAQ

1. I bought a share but I did not get it.
A. Your bid request has been sent and stored in exchange's queue. As soon as your bid becomes the best bid
and as we get an offer which suits your bid, you get your share of stocks. In simpler words, at this moment
there is no offer lesser than your bid.

2. I tried to sell a share, but I did not get any money.
A. Similarly, your sell/offer request is stored in a queue. As your offer becomes the best offer and then
when we get a bid which is more than your offer your share of stocks are sold.

3. I submitted a bid at Rs 100, but got the stocks at Rs 98. How?
A. You placed a Limit Order bid and not a Market Order. FreeMex does not support or allow Market Order.
Limit Order assures that you get your stocks at your SUITABLE price and not at exact (or best possible) price.
Your bid remains unaccessed till someone submits an offer which is lower than your bid. The price at
which you get the stocks, that is, the trade price is the AVERAGE of the bid and the offer.

4. I submitted an offer at Rs 100, but the stocks got sold at Rs 102. How?
A. You placed a Limit Order offer which assures that you sell your stocks at your SUITABLE price and not at exact
price. You sell your stocks only to bids higher than your offer at the average price of the bid and the offer.
This price then becomes the LTP (Last Traded Price).

5. I made a transaction on a stock but is not highlighted in the LTP.
A. The market is too big to be always affected by your transaction.

6. Price of a stock reached more than my offer price, but my stocks are not yet sold. Vice Versa.
A. The price of the stock reflects the Last Traded Price of the transaction and not the order you had placed.
And LTP is the average price of bid and offer.
